Additive Manufacturing at Mach Five

We run WLMD (Wire–Laser Metal Deposition) alongside our subtractive workflow, exploring where additive can unlock speed, complexity, and smarter material use across automotive.

What is WLMD?

6 focused lasers cocooned in argon gas form a melt pool while metal wire is fed precisely—building near-net shapes layer by layer with excellent metallurgical bonding.

Where We Use It Today

  • Rapid prototypes for brackets, housings, and complex ducting
  • Near-net blanks to reduce machining time on tough geometries
  • Gen 4 nuclear reactor bearings that face extreme enviroments
  • Precision part manufacturing at scale

Automotive Opportunities

  • Chassis hardware: aero mounts, brake ducts, chassis bracing, etc
  • Powertrain & cooling: enclosed heat exchangers, manifolds, braces
  • Factory: jigs, end-of-arm tooling, quick-change fixtures
